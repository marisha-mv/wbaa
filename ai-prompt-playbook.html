The Hook Formula

LinkedIn Email Subject Lines Ad Headlines
Framework
Great hooks follow a pattern: they create an open loop in the reader's mind. This prompt generates hooks using 5 proven formulas: Contrarian, Curiosity Gap, Specific Number, Before/After, and Direct Challenge. The result? Scroll-stopping first lines every time.
Prompt Template
You are a world-class copywriter who specializes in scroll-stopping hooks. Generate 10 hooks for a [content type: LinkedIn post/email/ad] about [topic]. Target audience: [describe your audience] Desired emotion: [curiosity/fear/excitement/urgency] Use these 5 hook formulas (2 of each): 1. Contrarian: Challenge a common belief 2. Curiosity Gap: Create an open loop 3. Specific Number: Use an unexpected stat 4. Before/After: Show transformation 5. Direct Challenge: Call out the reader For each hook: - Keep it under 12 words - Make it impossible to scroll past - Include a pattern interrupt Format: Number each hook and label which formula it uses.
Example Output
Sample hooks for "AI in marketing"

1. [Contrarian] "ChatGPT isn't making marketers obsolete. Bad marketers are."

2. [Curiosity Gap] "I replaced my $8K/month agency with a $20 tool. Here's the awkward part."

3. [Specific Number] "347 blog posts in 6 months. Same team. Here's the system."

2

Story-to-Lesson Framework

Blog Posts Newsletters Long-form Content
Framework
Stories are 22x more memorable than facts alone. This framework transforms any experience into a compelling narrative with a clear takeaway. Structure: Hook → Context → Conflict → Resolution → Lesson → Application. The story makes them care; the lesson makes it stick.
Prompt Template
You are a storytelling expert who transforms experiences into viral content. Turn this situation into a compelling story with a business lesson: The situation: [describe what happened - be specific] The lesson I want to convey: [your key takeaway] Target audience: [who is reading this] Platform: [LinkedIn/blog/newsletter] Desired length: [word count] Structure to follow: 1. HOOK: Start mid-action or with dialogue (no "I was..." openings) 2. CONTEXT: Set the scene in 2-3 sentences max 3. CONFLICT: What went wrong or created tension? 4. RESOLUTION: What happened? What did you do? 5. LESSON: The universal truth (make it about THEM) 6. APPLICATION: One specific action they can take today Tone: Conversational, honest, slightly self-deprecating Format: Short paragraphs, line breaks for readability
3

The Contrarian Take

Thought Leadership Viral Content LinkedIn
Framework
The fastest path to attention is disagreement. But contrarian content fails when it's contrarian for its own sake. This framework helps you find genuine counter-narratives that are both provocative AND defensible. The key: challenge the conventional wisdom, then back it up with evidence.
Prompt Template
You are a thought leader known for challenging industry assumptions. I want to write a contrarian take on: [topic/common belief in your industry] The conventional wisdom is: [what most people believe] My contrarian position is: [what you actually think] My evidence/experience: [why you believe this] Write a [LinkedIn post/blog intro/Twitter thread] that: 1. Opens by stating the conventional wisdom (make it feel obvious) 2. Pivots with "But here's what nobody talks about..." 3. Presents my contrarian view with 3 supporting points 4. Acknowledges the nuance (when the conventional wisdom IS right) 5. Ends with a question that invites discussion Rules: - Don't be contrarian just for engagement bait - Make the argument logical and evidence-based - Tone: Confident but not arrogant - Include a specific example or story
Example Output
Sample opening for "hustle culture"

"Everyone says work-life balance is the key to success.

Every productivity guru preaches boundaries. Every wellness brand sells you on 'rest.'

But here's what nobody talks about: The most successful people I know went through seasons of extreme imbalance. Seasons where they were ALL in.

The difference? They chose their imbalance intentionally..."

The Voice Training Technique

Brand Consistency All Content Types
Framework
Generic AI output happens because AI doesn't know YOUR voice. This two-step technique first trains AI on your brand's unique voice patterns, then uses that training for all future content. Run the Voice Analysis once, save it, and reference it in every prompt.
Prompt Template (Step 1: Voice Analysis)
Analyze the writing style of these 3 content samples and create a "Voice Profile" I can use in future prompts: [Paste 3 examples of YOUR best content - 500+ words each] Create a Voice Profile that includes: 1. TONE ATTRIBUTES (list 5 adjectives) 2. SENTENCE PATTERNS (short/long, simple/complex) 3. VOCABULARY PREFERENCES (formal/casual, jargon level) 4. SIGNATURE PHRASES (recurring expressions or patterns) 5. PERSPECTIVE (first person, second person, etc.) 6. EMOTIONAL RANGE (what emotions does this voice convey?) 7. WHAT THIS VOICE NEVER DOES (anti-patterns) Format the profile so I can paste it into future prompts.
Prompt Template (Step 2: Using the Voice)
Write [content type] about [topic]. Use this voice profile: [Paste your saved Voice Profile here] Additional context: [any specific requirements]

The Listicle Generator

Blog Posts Social Media Newsletters
Framework
Listicles get 2x more traffic than other blog formats because they're scannable and promise specific value. But most listicles are shallow. This framework creates listicles with depth: each item has context, example, and actionable takeaway. Quality AND quantity.
Prompt Template
Create a listicle: "[Number] [Topic] for [Audience]" Context about my audience: [what they struggle with, their level] For each list item, include: 1. A compelling subheadline (curiosity-driven, not generic) 2. WHY this matters (2-3 sentences of context) 3. A specific example or mini-case study 4. One actionable takeaway they can implement today Format requirements: - Items should be ordered from most impactful to least - Include a brief intro (3 sentences max) that hooks the reader - End with a conclusion that ties the items together - Total length: [word count] Avoid: - Generic advice ("be consistent," "work hard") - Anything they've read 100 times before - Filler content to hit the number

Landing Page Copy Generator

Sales Pages Product Launches Lead Magnets
Framework
Every high-converting landing page follows the same structure: Hero (grab attention) → Problem (agitate pain) → Solution (present your offer) → Proof (build trust) → CTA (drive action). This prompt generates copy for each section with the psychological triggers baked in.
Prompt Template
You are a conversion copywriter who has written pages generating $10M+ in sales. Create landing page copy for: [product/service name] PRODUCT DETAILS: - What it is: [describe the offering] - Price point: [price] - Target audience: [who is this for] - Main pain point solved: [the core problem] - Key differentiator: [what makes this unique] Generate copy for each section: 1. HERO SECTION - Headline (10 words max, benefit-focused) - Subheadline (clarify what it is + key outcome) - 3 bullet points (specific benefits with outcomes) - CTA button text 2. PROBLEM SECTION - Header that calls out their pain - 3-4 pain points they're experiencing (be specific) - Agitate: what happens if this isn't solved? 3. SOLUTION SECTION - Position your offer as the bridge - "What if you could..." framing - 3 key features translated to benefits 4. SOCIAL PROOF SECTION - Testimonial prompt structure - Stats/numbers to highlight 5. CTA SECTION - Headline creating urgency - Risk reversal statement - Final CTA button text Tone: Confident, conversational, zero fluff
7

CRO Audit Prompt

Conversion Optimization Landing Pages
Framework
Before you rewrite, analyze. This prompt audits existing landing pages for conversion leaks using a systematic checklist: clarity, credibility, anxiety reducers, motivation triggers, and friction points. Paste your page copy and get specific, actionable fixes.
Prompt Template
You are a CRO specialist who has optimized 500+ landing pages. Audit this landing page for conversion leaks: [Paste the full landing page copy OR provide URL] Context: - Target audience: [who] - Goal: [lead capture/purchase/signup] - Current conversion rate (if known): [%] Analyze using this framework: 1. CLARITY (0-10 score) - Can I understand what this is in 5 seconds? - Is the value proposition crystal clear? - Issues found + specific fixes 2. CREDIBILITY (0-10 score) - Is there enough social proof? - Are claims backed by evidence? - Issues found + specific fixes 3. ANXIETY REDUCERS (0-10 score) - Are objections addressed? - Is there a guarantee/risk reversal? - Issues found + specific fixes 4. MOTIVATION (0-10 score) - Does it create urgency? - Are benefits emotional + specific? - Issues found + specific fixes 5. FRICTION (0-10 score) - Is the CTA clear and prominent? - Are there unnecessary form fields? - Issues found + specific fixes End with: TOP 3 HIGHEST-IMPACT CHANGES (prioritized by effort vs. impact)

Ad Creative Variations

Meta Ads LinkedIn Ads Google Ads
Framework
One product, multiple angles. The best ad accounts test different emotional appeals: fear of missing out, desire for status, pain avoidance, aspiration, and social proof. This prompt generates variations across all angles so you can let the data decide what works.
Prompt Template
You are a performance marketer who manages $1M+/month in ad spend. Create ad variations for: [product/service] Target audience: [demographics, psychographics] Platform: [Meta/LinkedIn/Google] Objective: [awareness/leads/sales] Generate 2 ads for EACH angle (10 total): ANGLE 1: PAIN AGITATION - Headline focused on the problem they're facing - Body copy that twists the knife (ethically) - CTA: urgency-based ANGLE 2: ASPIRATION - Headline focused on the outcome/transformation - Body copy painting the "after" picture - CTA: desire-based ANGLE 3: SOCIAL PROOF - Headline featuring a result or testimonial - Body copy with specifics (numbers, names) - CTA: FOMO-based ANGLE 4: CONTRARIAN/PATTERN INTERRUPT - Headline that challenges assumptions - Body copy that reframes the problem - CTA: curiosity-based ANGLE 5: DIRECT/LOGICAL - Headline stating exactly what it is - Body copy with features → benefits - CTA: straightforward Format each ad: - Primary text (125 chars max for feed) - Headline (40 chars max) - Description (30 chars max) - CTA button recommendation

Email Sequence Builder

Lead Nurturing Onboarding Abandoned Cart
Framework
Great email sequences take readers on a journey: from stranger to buyer. Each email has one job, and together they build momentum. This framework uses psychological triggers (reciprocity, authority, scarcity, social proof, commitment) mapped to a 5-email arc.
Prompt Template
You are an email marketing strategist with a 45%+ open rate track record. Create a 5-email nurture sequence for: [product/service] Context: - How they opted in: [lead magnet/webinar/etc] - Their main problem: [pain point] - End goal: [what action you want them to take] - Timeline: [days between emails] Email sequence structure: EMAIL 1: THE WELCOME (Reciprocity) - Deliver on the opt-in promise - Quick win they can implement immediately - Set expectations for the sequence - Soft CTA: reply with their #1 challenge EMAIL 2: THE STORY (Connection) - Your origin story related to their problem - Show you understand their struggle - Position yourself as a guide, not a hero - CTA: consume a piece of content EMAIL 3: THE PROOF (Authority + Social Proof) - Case study or testimonial deep-dive - Specific results with context - Address the "but will it work for me?" objection - CTA: check out [offer] if ready EMAIL 4: THE OBJECTION HANDLER (Address Fears) - Call out the reasons they haven't bought - Dismantle each objection with logic + story - Risk reversal / guarantee details - CTA: direct offer EMAIL 5: THE DEADLINE (Scarcity + Urgency) - Final reminder with real urgency - Recap of benefits - What they'll miss if they don't act - Clear, single CTA For each email provide: - Subject line (+ 1 alternative) - Preview text - Full email body - P.S. line

UI/UX Copywriting Prompt

Product Interfaces Onboarding Flows Error Messages
Framework
UI copy is the most underrated conversion lever. Every button, tooltip, and error message either builds trust or creates friction. This prompt generates microcopy that's clear, human, and action-oriented - turning confusing interfaces into intuitive experiences.
Prompt Template
You are a UX writer at a top tech company known for delightful interfaces. Create UI copy for: [product/feature name] Product context: - What the product does: [brief description] - Target user: [who uses this] - Brand voice: [professional/playful/minimal/friendly] Generate copy for these elements: 1. BUTTONS & CTAs - Primary action button (3 options) - Secondary action (3 options) - Destructive action (delete/cancel) (2 options) 2. EMPTY STATES - When there's no data yet (headline + body + CTA) - When search returns no results 3. SUCCESS MESSAGES - After completing main action - After saving/updating 4. ERROR MESSAGES - Form validation errors (5 common ones) - System errors (apologetic + actionable) - Permission errors 5. ONBOARDING TOOLTIPS - First-time user hints (3 key features) - Progress indicators copy 6. CONFIRMATION MODALS - Before destructive action - Before major change Rules: - Max 8 words for buttons - Max 15 words for tooltips - Be specific about what happens next - Never blame the user - Always provide a path forward

Design Brief Generator

Briefing Designers Creating Mockups AI Image Generation
Framework
Great design starts with a great brief. This prompt translates business objectives into concrete design requirements: mood, visual references, constraints, and success metrics. Use it to brief human designers or generate detailed prompts for AI design tools.
Prompt Template
You are a creative director who has briefed award-winning campaigns. Create a design brief for: [project type: landing page/social ad/email/etc] Business context: - Company: [company name + what they do] - Campaign goal: [awareness/conversions/engagement] - Target audience: [demographics + psychographics] - Key message: [the one thing they should remember] - Existing brand guidelines: [colors/fonts/style or "none"] Generate a comprehensive brief: 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW - Objective in one sentence - Success metrics - Timeline/deadlines 2. AUDIENCE INSIGHTS - Who they are (beyond demographics) - What they care about - Visual preferences of this audience 3. CREATIVE DIRECTION - Mood/feeling (3-5 adjectives) - Visual style references (describe 3) - Color palette recommendations - Typography style - Imagery style (photo/illustration/abstract) 4. CONTENT REQUIREMENTS - Required elements (logo, CTA, etc.) - Copy to include - Hierarchy of information 5. TECHNICAL SPECS - Dimensions/formats needed - File requirements - Platform-specific considerations 6. INSPIRATION & REFERENCES - 3 examples of designs that hit the right tone - What to avoid (anti-references) 7. REVIEW CRITERIA - How we'll evaluate success - Key stakeholder concerns to address

User Journey Mapper

Product Strategy Marketing Funnels Customer Experience
Framework
Customer journeys reveal where people drop off and why. This prompt maps the entire experience from first touch to loyal customer, identifying pain points, emotional states, and opportunities at each stage. Use it to find the biggest levers for growth.
Prompt Template
You are a customer experience strategist who has optimized journeys for 8-figure brands. Map the customer journey for: [product/service] Context: - Business model: [B2B/B2C/SaaS/E-commerce/etc] - Primary acquisition channel: [how they find you] - Average sales cycle: [time from discovery to purchase] - Price point: [price] Create a journey map with these stages: 1. AWARENESS - How they discover you (channels) - What triggers their search - Their emotional state - Questions in their mind - Content needed at this stage - Key metrics to track 2. CONSIDERATION - What they're comparing you against - Their evaluation criteria - Pain points / friction - Emotional state - Content needed at this stage - Key metrics to track 3. DECISION - Final triggers to purchase - Objections that remain - Pain points / friction - Emotional state - What tips them over the edge - Key metrics to track 4. ONBOARDING - First experience after purchase - "Aha moment" - when do they get value? - Pain points / friction - Emotional state - Support needed - Key metrics to track 5. RETENTION - What keeps them engaged - Risk of churn indicators - Expansion opportunities - Emotional state - Key metrics to track 6. ADVOCACY - What triggers referrals - How to encourage sharing - Emotional state - Key metrics to track End with: TOP 5 OPPORTUNITIES (where the biggest gaps/levers are)
